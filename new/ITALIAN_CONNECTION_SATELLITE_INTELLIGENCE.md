## 2. The Leonardo S.p.A. Ecosystem

**Leonardo S.p.A.** (formerly Finmeccanica) is a prominent Italian multinational specializing in aerospace, defense, and security. It is **30.2% owned by the Italian Ministry of Economy and Finance**, making it a de facto state actor. Its operations in Costa Rica are conducted primarily through two key joint ventures:

### 2.1 Telespazio (67% Leonardo / 33% Thales)

- **Role:** Satellite services and ground segment operations.
- **Key Contract ("The Cadastral Anchor"):** In March 2020, Telespazio Argentina (a subsidiary) won a **$20 million contract** from the Costa Rican government to perform a massive cadastral survey.
  - **Scope:** Mapping 50% of the country (over one million land parcels).
  - **Implication:** To fulfill this contract, Telespazio requires precise, high-resolution satellite imagery and aerial survey data of half the nation's territory. This legitimizes the continuous orbital observation of Costa Rica by Italian assets. The "cadastral" mission provides a perfect cover for comprehensive terrain mapping.

### 2.2 e-GEOS (80% Telespazio / 20% Italian Space Agency)

- **Role:** Commercialization of COSMO-SkyMed data and geospatial intelligence.
- **Capability:** e-GEOS operates the platforms that process the raw radar data from the satellites. They offer services for "defense and intelligence," "maritime surveillance," and "critical infrastructure monitoring."
- **The "CLEOS" Platform:** e-GEOS, in partnership with Accenture, developed the CLEOS platform to process satellite data in the cloud. This suggests that Costa Rican data collected under the cadastral contract may be processed in third-party cloud environments (potentially AWS or Azure) accessible to Italian intelligence analysts.

## 3. The COSMO-SkyMed Constellation: The "All-Seeing Eye"

The **COSMO-SkyMed (CSG)** system is the backbone of this surveillance architecture. Funded by the Italian MoD and the Italian Space Agency (ASI), it is a **dual-use (civilian/military) X-band SAR constellation**.

| **Identification Capability** | Vehicle types, fencing structures, encampments |

| **Operational Mode** | Near-real-time monitoring of specific targets |

### 3.2 Verified Operational History in the Region

- **2010 Border Dispute:** The European External Action Service (EEAS) activated COSMO-SkyMed services to monitor the Costa Rica–Nicaragua border dispute (Isla Calero/Portillos).
  - **Significance:** Proves the system is already "tasked" for security and intelligence missions over Costa Rican territory. The precedent exists for using these assets to monitor geopolitical friction points in the region.
- **Emergency Mapping:** The system is regularly activated for disaster response (e.g., floods, volcanoes). This humanitarian mandate ensures that local authorities are accustomed to sharing data with Italian providers, lowering resistance to their presence.

- **The "Dual-Use" Loophole:** Under Italian law and the dual-use nature of the COSMO-SkyMed program, the Italian MoD has priority access to this data.
- **Hypothesis:** The "Cadastral Survey" is effectively a **Bulk Collection** operation. By mapping land ownership, boundaries, and infrastructure for the Costa Rican registry, Italian intelligence gains a high-fidelity "Digital Twin" of the country's physical terrain, which can be shared with NATO allies (including the US) under intelligence-sharing frameworks.

## 5. The "Sparkle" Submarine Cable Link

- **Pan American Crossing (PAC):** Sparkle is a consortium owner/capacity holder on major cable systems like the Pan American (PAN-AM) and Americas-II cables.
- **Routing Anomaly:** Traceroutes from Costa Rica often show traffic hopping through Miami (NAP of the Americas) before routing to Europe via Sparkle's network (AS6762).
- **Strategic Chokepoint:** Sparkle's infrastructure in Panama and Miami facilitates the transport of data from Central America to Italy. If Italian intelligence (or their US partners) wished to tap communications, the "landing stations" and "points of presence" managed by Sparkle would be the logical physical access points.
