## VI. THE BAYESIAN THREAT PRIOR

### 5.1 Bayesian Updating

Every encounter with $\mathcal{B}$ is an implicit Bayesian update. The observer's brain processes:

- **Prior**: $P_T(n-1)$ — current belief that $\mathcal{B}$ is a threat
- **Likelihood**: $P(\text{no harm} \mid \text{threat})$ — probability of surviving exposure if $\mathcal{B}$ is actually dangerous
- **Evidence**: $P(\text{no harm} \mid \text{safe})$ — probability of surviving exposure if $\mathcal{B}$ is safe (≈ 1.0)

By Bayes' theorem, after each uneventful exposure:

$$P_T(n) = \frac{P_T(n-1) \cdot P(\text{no harm} \mid \text{threat})}{P_T(n-1) \cdot P(\text{no harm} \mid \text{threat}) + (1 - P_T(n-1)) \cdot 1.0}$$

For a building that *might* be something sinister:
- $P_T(0) = 0.001$ (a 6-year-old's baseline: 0.1% chance any building is "bad")
- $P(\text{no harm} \mid \text{threat}) = 0.999$ (a surveillance post wouldn't harm you — it just watches)

This is the **genius of the design**: a surveillance post has a near-unity probability of producing "no harm" evidence even when it IS a threat. The Bayesian update is:

$$P_T(n) = \frac{0.001 \times 0.999^n}{0.001 \times 0.999^n + 0.999}$$

After $n = 3{,}645$ exposures:

$$P_T(3645) = \frac{0.001 \times 0.999^{3645}}{0.001 \times 0.999^{3645} + 0.999}$$

$$0.999^{3645} = e^{3645 \times \ln(0.999)} = e^{3645 \times (-0.001001)} = e^{-3.648} \approx 0.02604$$

$$P_T(3645) = \frac{0.001 \times 0.02604}{0.001 \times 0.02604 + 0.999} = \frac{2.604 \times 10^{-5}}{0.99903}$$

$$\boxed{P_T(3645) \approx 2.6 \times 10^{-5}}$$

The probability that Echo's brain assigns to "that building is a threat" after 3,645 uneventful exposures is **0.0026%** — roughly 1 in 38,000.
