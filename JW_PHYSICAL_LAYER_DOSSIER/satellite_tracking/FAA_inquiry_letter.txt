=========================================

My monitoring station uses calibrated audio spectrum analysis (48 kHz
sample rate, 24-bit resolution, 1024-point FFT). I have isolated two
persistent signal artifacts that correlate with orbital windows:

A. The 46.875 Hz Signature (System Clock Feedthrough)
------------------------------------------------------
I have detected a persistent narrowband signal at exactly 46.875 Hz.

  Technical Context: 48,000 Hz / 1024 bins = 46.875 Hz

While this is the fundamental frequency resolution of standard FFT
processing, the signal power varies between 2.30 dB and 16.29 dB
above the noise floor depending on satellite proximity. Harmonics are
present at:
  - 93.750 Hz  (2nd harmonic, +3.4 dB)
  - 140.625 Hz (3rd harmonic, +5.3 dB)

Hypothesis: Hardware clock feedthrough or beat frequency from a Pulse
Repetition Frequency (PRF) synchronized to standard DSP timing rates,
potentially originating from ground-based telemetry equipment or PLC
(Power Line Communication) repeaters.

B. The 24.2 Hz ELF Transient
------------------------------

breaker resulted in a 98.4% signal drop (2,369 → 38 units). This
confirms the building's wiring is acting as a receiving antenna
(inductive coupling), picking up Ground Potential Rise (GPR) or
