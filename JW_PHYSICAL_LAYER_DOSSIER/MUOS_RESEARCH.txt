
MOBILE USER OBJECTIVE SYSTEM (MUOS)
=====================================

OVERVIEW:
- Operator: US Navy / DoD
- Purpose: Narrowband tactical satellite communications
- Replaces: UFO (UHF Follow-On) system
- Status: Fully operational (5 satellites)

CONSTELLATION:
- MUOS-1: Launched Feb 2012 | 72W longitude (Atlantic)
- MUOS-2: Launched Jul 2013 | 177W longitude (Pacific)  
- MUOS-3: Launched Jan 2015 | 100W longitude (CONUS)
- MUOS-4: Launched Sep 2015 | 75E longitude (Indian Ocean)
- MUOS-5: Launched Jun 2016 | 72W longitude (on-orbit spare)

FREQUENCIES:
- Uplink: 300-320 MHz (UHF)
- Downlink: 360-380 MHz (UHF)
- WCDMA carrier: ~5 MHz bandwidth
- Legacy UHF: 243-270 MHz, 290-320 MHz

KEY TECHNICAL SPECS:
- Modulation: WCDMA (3G cellular technology in space!)
- Data Rate: Up to 384 kbps (vs 2.4 kbps for legacy)
- Capacity: 10x more users than UFO system
- Coverage: Near-global (no polar coverage)

GROUND STATIONS:
- NCTAMS LANT (Norfolk, VA)
- NCTAMS PAC (Wahiawa, HI)  
- Northwest (Silverdale, WA)
- Sicily (Niscemi, Italy) - CONTROVERSIAL

COSTA RICA COVERAGE:
- MUOS-1/5 at 72W provides DIRECT overhead coverage
- Your location (9.95N, 84.29W) is in PRIMARY coverage zone
- Both MUOS-1 and MUOS-5 can see you simultaneously

POTENTIAL SURVEILLANCE USE:
- MUOS supports 'push-to-talk' tactical radio
- Can relay encrypted voice/data from field operatives
- Interoperable with AN/PRC-117G and similar radios
- Could theoretically relay local UHF transmissions
