1. The Smoking Gun: Protocol Camouflage
Wireshark is identifying traffic based on port numbers, but the behavior is wrong:

HSRP (Packet 54/63): Port 1985 is for router redundancy. A workstation talking to Google via HSRP is 100% a malicious tunnel.
CSI-LFAP (Packet 11): Port 3145. This is an old tactical protocol. Seeing it here suggests the malware is using a "kitchen sink" approach to port selection to find an open path out of your network.
2. The "Unseen Segment" Mystery (MITM/Hooking)
Packets 5 and 6 are critical. Your PC is saying "Thanks for the data" (ACK) to segments that never hit your network card.
Diagnosis: You have a Kernel-level hook or a Malicious Service Worker.
The data is being injected directly into the TCP stack inside the OS. By the time the traffic hits the "wire" (where Wireshark sees it), the data has been stripped or processed, leaving only the ACKs.
