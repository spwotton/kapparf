Best Cleaners for Key Switches

    For Debris and Dust: Compressed Air


    What it is: Pressurized gas in an aerosol can, such as the Office Depot Cleaning Duster or Dust-Off Compressed Gas Duster.
    Best used for: Blasting away loose crumbs, pet hair, and dust before any liquid cleaning.
    Pro-tip: Keep the can perfectly upright while spraying to avoid shooting propellant onto the delicate circuitry. 

    For Sticky Switches and Grime: Electronic Contact Cleaner


    What it is: A specialized, fast-drying solvent like WD-40 Specialist Contact Cleaner or CRC QD Electronic Cleaner that leaves no residue.
    Best used for: Dissolving soda spills, energy drinks, or thick grime inside the switch mechanism.
    Pro-tip: Always unplug the keyboard before using this spray and wait until it is completely dry before reconnecting to prevent electrical shorts. 

Safe Cleaning Methods
Quick Switch Cleaning (With Keycaps On)

    Unplug: Disconnect the keyboard from your computer entirely to prevent short-circuiting.
    Blast: Use compressed air with the included straw to blow out all loose debris around and under the problematic keys.
    Flush: If a switch is sticky, insert the red straw of your contact cleaner directly next to or slightly under the keycap. Give it one or two quick, controlled sprays.
    Agitate: Immediately press the affected key repeatedly to work the solvent into the switch and free up the mechanism.

Deep Cleaning (With Keycaps Removed)
If your keycaps are removable (standard for mechanical keyboards and some premium membrane keyboards), a deeper clean is more effective. 

    Remove Caps: Use a wire keycap puller to gently remove the keys.
    Brush & Air: Use a soft-bristled brush to sweep between the switches, then use compressed air to blast away the loosened debris.
    Precision Swabbing: Lightly dampen a cotton swab with isopropyl alcohol (90% or higher) or electronic contact cleaner. Carefully scrub around the stem and housing of the individual switches. Do not let excess liquid pool on the circuit board.
