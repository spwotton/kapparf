whas difference one is vpn one is firewall?
15 days ago

Good question. They do two completely different things:

Firewall (UFW) = the bouncer at your door. It blocks anyone from connecting TO your machine on specific ports. Without it, anyone on the hotel WiFi could try to reach into your laptop through SNMP, Modbus, mDNS, etc. The firewall says "no, that port is closed."

VPN (WARP) = an armored tunnel for everything going OUT. When you browse, load a page, send a message — all that traffic goes through the hotel's WiFi access point. Without a VPN, whoever runs that UniFi AP can see every domain you visit. With the VPN, all they see is encrypted gibberish going to Cloudflare.

You need both:

    Firewall without VPN = your ports are closed but your traffic is visible
    VPN without firewall = your traffic is encrypted but your ports are open to attack
    Both together = ports closed AND traffic encrypted. Ghost mode.

That's what the hardening script does — sets up both in one shot.
