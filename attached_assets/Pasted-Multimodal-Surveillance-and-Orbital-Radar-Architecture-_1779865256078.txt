7.2 Public Procurement Exceptions: Law 7494 and Law 9986
To circumvent these civilian privacy requirements, the operational framework relies on the sovereign authority of the Costa Rican state, specifically utilizing the Ley de Contratación Administrativa.
Under the previous regime (Law No. 7494), public contracts were required to respect strict equality of participation, with exceptions carved out for sole suppliers or matters of manifest public interest. However, under the updated framework of Law No. 9986, Article 3 explicitly delineates exceptions for contractual activity subjected to special procedures via international agreements, as well as contractual activity developed between entities of public law. [1]
Because Telespazio's survey is executed under the direct mandate of the Registro Nacional, and funded organically through national property taxes (Ley Impuesto sobre Bienes Inmuebles, No. 7509), the raw geometric and cartographic data is shielded. The placement of corner cubes and the highly specific geodetic coordinates collected during the "verificación de campo" are classified as technical artifacts of a sovereign infrastructure project, rather than personal data subject to Law 8968. Consequently, Freedom of Information (FOIA) or public transparency requests aimed at extracting the specific coordinates of the calibration array, or questioning the exact nature of the high-frequency telemetry routed over the Sparkle network, are routinely denied under national security or critical infrastructure exemptions. [1]
